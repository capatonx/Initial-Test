Documents & Money

Clothing & Layers
  • Lightweight merino or thermal mid-layer — essential for Ben Lomond, Roy’s Peak, and Fiordland evenings
  • Windproof fleece or light down jacket — summit temperatures run 5–10 °C colder than Queenstown town
  • Waterproof hardshell rain jacket — mandatory for Fiordland, useful in Queenstown and on the Milford Drive
  • Smart-casual dinner wear for Rātā and Cabot Lodge evenings — hiking gear is fine everywhere else
  • Swimwear (3–4 pieces) — five full Fiji days in and around water; sand and salt take a toll

  • UV-rated rashguard — for diving, snorkeling, and Cloud 9 (UV is extreme in November Fiji)
  • Comfortable walking shoes for town days and vineyard touring (Waiheke, Queenstown)
Activity & Hiking Gear
  • Waterproof hiking boots — required for Ben Lomond, Roy’s Peak, and Milford Drive stop walks; broken-in before travel
  • Wool or synthetic hiking socks (3–4 pairs) — avoid cotton on multi-hour ascents
  • Daypack (20–25 L) — for hike days and heli-hike; doubles as a beach bag in Fiji
  • Trekking poles — optional but genuinely helpful on Ben Lomond’s steeper descent sections
  • Dry bag (10–20 L) — for Kaituna rafting and Fiji boat days
  • Spare set of dry clothes in a separate bag for after the Kaituna rafting session
  • Reef fins and snorkel mask — resort hire is available at Momi Bay but your own gear is more comfortable for multiple sessions
  • Wetsuits and helmets are supplied by Kaituna rafting operators and Aqua-Trek — no need to pack your own
Sun & Skin Protection
  • Broad-spectrum SPF 50+ sunscreen — UV is high from November 8 onward in New Zealand; reapply every 2 hrs
  • Reef-safe (mineral) SPF 50+ sunscreen — required for diving and snorkeling in Fiji to protect coral ecosystems
  • Wide-brim sun hat — essential for Hobbiton (open farmland), both NZ hike days, and every Fiji activity day
  • Polarised sunglasses — critical for the fishing charter; also valuable on the Milford Drive and hike days
  • SPF lip balm
Health & Wellbeing
  • DEET 40%+ insect repellent — non-negotiable for Fiordland. Sandfly bites are immediate, intensely itchy, and last for days. Apply before going outdoors at Cabot Lodge, on the Milford Drive, and near any still water.
  • Motion sickness tablets — for the Kaituna rafting session and offshore Fiji fishing charter if prone
  • Seasickness wristbands or patches — backup for the Milford Sound cruise and Reel Fiji charter
  • Compression socks — for the ~17.5 hr JFK→AKL flight and the return NAN→LAX leg
  • Travel neck pillow — the most impactful long-haul comfort item for the outbound leg
  • All prescription medications — carry in hand luggage with documentation; do not pack in checked bags
  • Basic first-aid kit: blister plasters (hike days), antihistamine cream (sandflies), pain relief, antiseptic wipes
  • Rehydration sachets — useful after rafting, big hike days, and Fiji heat and humidity
Electronics & Adaptors
  • Power adaptor — Type I (three-prong angled flat pins). Same plug type for both New Zealand and Fiji. North American plugs do not fit without an adaptor.
  • Portable battery / power bank — essential for Ben Lomond, Roy’s Peak, and heli-hike days where there’s no access to power for 8–10 hrs
  • Waterproof phone case or pouch — for the Kaituna rafting session, Fiji boat days, and Milford Sound rain

Notes & Tips

Recommended routing: Air New Zealand operates a non-stop JFK → AKL service (flight NZ2, ~17.5 hrs). Departures are typically evening from JFK Terminal 1; you cross the International Date Line and arrive Auckland on Sunday morning. This is the smoothest option — no connection stress, no domestic transfer.

Arrival note: You land Auckland, clear customs and biosecurity, then make your way to the Downtown Ferry Terminal for the Waiheke ferry (~35 min). Budget 2–3 hrs from touchdown to ferry boarding.

Waiheke Island

Home on Waiheke • 2 Nights

Waiheke Island, Auckland
Sun, Nov 8 – Tue, Nov 10 2 Nights

Location Notes

Waiheke is your soft landing: wine, views, low friction. No rental car needed here. Best approach is ferry + local transport.

Travel Logistics

  • Auckland Airport → Ferry Terminal: SkyBus express (~1 hr, ~NZD 24 pp) drops directly at Britomart, a short walk from Pier 1. Alternatively, taxi (~NZD 65–80, ~45 min) or pre-booked transfer. SkyBus is the most straightforward option with luggage.
  • Ferry — Fullers360: Pier 1, Quay Street → Matiatia Wharf, Waiheke Island. ~35 min. Ferries run every 30–60 min throughout the day (first ~6:30 am, last ~11:30 pm). Book at fullers.co.nz ↗ or buy at the terminal.
  • On Waiheke: No rental car needed. The Waiheke Bus Company covers Matiatia → Oneroa → Palm Beach. Taxis and app-based rideshares available at the wharf and in Oneroa village. E-bikes and regular bikes available for hire at Matiatia Wharf — ideal for the wine touring day.
  • Departure (Nov 10): Ferry from Matiatia back to downtown Auckland. Aim for a mid-morning departure (~9–10 am) to allow time to pick up the rental car and drive to Raglan without rushing.

Weather in November

Late spring transitioning to early summer — Waiheke is one of New Zealand’s sunniest spots. Daytime temperatures 19–23 °C (66–73 °F) with typically 7–8 hours of sunshine daily. UV is high from the start of November; sunscreen every morning is essential.

Occasional afternoon showers are possible but rarely prolonged. Evenings are mild and long — daylight stretches to around 8:30–9 pm, making outdoor dining and evening vineyard visits feel relaxed rather than rushed.

What to pack: Light layers for evenings, high-SPF sunscreen and sunglasses for wine-touring days, comfortable walking shoes. A compact umbrella is handy but rarely essential.

Notes & Tips

Keep Day 1 intentionally light to absorb the travel day. Good evening to stay close to the property.

Keep the wine day curated rather than overpacked — three to four stops is the right pace.

Raglan

Home in Raglan • 2 Nights

Raglan, Waikato
Tue, Nov 10 – Thu, Nov 12 2 Nights

Location Notes & Accommodation Options

Raglan is your surf-and-slow-down stop — New Zealand’s most iconic surf town, with a relaxed west-coast feel that’s a deliberate contrast to Waiheke’s vineyard polish. The rental car you pick up in Auckland covers all Raglan and onward transport. Raglan has no luxury hotels — the right call here is a well-chosen private home.

Accommodation still to book — priority item. Options:

  • Private holiday home via Bookabach — Best fit for a honeymoon stop. Search for harbour-view homes on Whaanga Road or Marine Parade for panoramic views over Raglan Harbour and the town. Homes sleep 2–4 and suit the stay well. Budget ~NZD 350–550/night. Search early — quality homes book out months ahead for the November summer season. bookabach.co.nz ↗ or holidayhouses.co.nz ↗
  • Solscape Eco Retreat — Raglan’s most characterful accommodation: handcrafted yurts and rondavels on a hillside with surf-break views. More eclectic than luxurious, but genuinely atmospheric. A good fallback if a private home isn’t available. solscape.co.nz ↗
  • Local B&B or Airbnb — A handful of well-reviewed small stays operate in and around Raglan township. Prioritise something close to the village with an outdoor space. Airbnb ↗

Travel Logistics

  • Ferry back to Auckland (Nov 10): Fullers360, Matiatia Wharf → Pier 1, Quay Street (~35 min). Aim for a 9–10 am ferry — leaves time to pick up the car and arrive Raglan for early afternoon. Book via the Fullers360 app or at the terminal.
  • Rental car pickup: Pick up in central Auckland — CBD branches (Victoria St, Quay St area) are a short walk or taxi from the ferry terminal. Options: Go Rentals (NZ-based, competitive), Budget, Avis, Europcar. Book automatic transmission — you’ll be driving on the left for the first time. Recommend a compact SUV (Toyota RAV4 class) — handles NZ roads well and right for the Cabot Lodge access road later in the trip.
  • Drive to Raglan — ~1.5–2 hrs: South on SH1 to Hamilton, then west on SH23 to Raglan. Mostly straightforward motorway driving. GPS recommended. Note: roundabouts and motorway on-ramps are where left-side driving trips people up most — take the first stretch at a comfortable pace. Fuel up before leaving Auckland.
  • Parking in Raglan: Free street parking throughout town — no issues.
  • Departure (Nov 12): Drive Raglan → Hobbiton (~1.5 hrs via SH23/SH29). See Rotorua section for full day timing.

Day 2 — Wed, Nov 11
Morning

Ngarunui Beach (Ocean Beach) is the main patrolled black-sand stretch — ideal for beginners and body surfing, with lifeguard patrols from Labour Weekend onward. For the famous Raglan left-handers, Manu Bay is the classic point break (intermediate to advanced); Whale Bay is a second left a short walk further south. Surf lessons and board hire: Raglan Surfing School (~NZD 75–95 for a 2 hr lesson including board and wetsuit hire) or Surfing with Wings — both operate from Ngarunui Beach and are the right call for a first session. Water temperature is ~17 °C in November: a 3–4 mm wetsuit is essential and is included with lesson hire.

Midday

Waireinga / Bridal Veil Falls — a 55 m plunge waterfall set in a native bush gully, ~22 km south of Raglan. From town, head east on Wainui Road then turn south on Kawhia Road and follow the signs (~25 min drive). The carpark is small — aim to arrive before noon. Two viewing tracks from the carpark: the upper platform (10 min each way, gentle grade) gives the full top-to-bottom view of the drop; a steeper track continues to the base (~15 min) where the spray and scale become much more immediate. Dense nikau palms and native tree ferns line the lower track — photogenic even on an overcast day. Total time including both platforms: about 1 hr.

Return via Raglan for a late lunch at Volvo Café (strong coffee, great food) or The Shack before a relaxed final afternoon on the west coast. Early night is worth protecting — tomorrow is one of the most time-compressed days of the trip.

Relaxed dinner. Pack for tomorrow.
Dining options: Repeat favorite or keep dinner simple near home.

Weather in November

West coast exposure means Raglan runs slightly cooler and breezier than Auckland. Daytime temperatures 16–21 °C (61–70 °F), with regular offshore winds that make conditions lively for surfing.

November marks the start of reliable surf season — consistent Tasman swells arriving from the southwest. Check surf forecasts the evening before (Magic Seaweed or Surf-Forecast.com) to plan the session around tide and swell size.

What to pack: A light windproof layer for the coast. Wetsuit hire is available locally — a 3–4 mm full suit is right for November water temperatures (~17 °C). The Bridal Veil Falls trail is well shaded; a light jacket is welcome for the walk.

Notes & Tips

Good day to settle into driving on the left. Keep the evening easy.

Day 2 should feel flexible and low-pressure. Keep timing loose around surf conditions.

Rotorua

Regent of Rotorua • 1 Night

Rotorua, Bay of Plenty
Thu, Nov 12 – Fri, Nov 13 1 Night

Location Notes

This is a logistics-driven stop. Regent of Rotorua is a strong fit — comfortable night with better dining access and an easy airport departure the next morning. Goal: Hobbiton + rafting + early flight.

Travel Logistics

  • Raglan → Hobbiton (~1.5 hrs): Head east on SH23, north on SH1 briefly, then east on SH29 toward Matamata. Hobbiton Movie Set is ~10 min from Matamata town. Depart Raglan by 7:30 am to make a 9:30 am tour start. Book in advance at hobbitontours.com ↗ — November is peak season and tours sell out weeks ahead.
  • Hobbiton → Kaituna rafting (~1 hr): Drive south via SH29/SH5 to Rotorua. Recommended operators: Kaituna Cascades and K2K Rafting both run the Kaituna River and the famous 7 m Tutea Falls drop — the highest commercially rafted waterfall in New Zealand. Sessions run ~2 hrs including safety briefing. Depart Hobbiton by 12:30 pm to reach the put-in for a 2:00–2:30 pm session.
  • Rafting → Regent of Rotorua (~20 min): The Regent is on Hinemaru Street in central Rotorua — easy to find. Expect to arrive ~5:30 pm. Gear up for a shower, dinner, and early pack for departure.
  • Departure morning (Nov 13): Rotorua Airport (ROT) is ~20 min from the hotel. Return rental car at the airport — Budget and Avis desks on-site. Allow 1.5 hrs before your flight. Air New Zealand operates ROT→AKL, connecting to ZQN. Confirm timing when booking — typically a morning departure to catch the Queenstown connection.

Activities

Day 1 — Thu, Nov 12
Morning

Depart Raglan by 7:30 am. Drive east on SH23 to Hamilton, then east on SH29 toward Matamata (~1.5 hrs total). The Hobbiton Movie Set entrance is at 501 Buckland Road, Hinuera — ~10 min from Matamata township; follow signs from the main road. Allow 15 min to check in and join your group before the tour starts.

The standard tour runs approximately 2 hours with a guide leading groups across 12 acres of the Shire set: 44 Hobbit holes (a mix of full-scale and miniature-scale), Bilbo’s garden, Sam’s house, the Mill, the Party Tree, and Bag End at the top of the hill. The tour concludes at the Green Dragon Inn with a complimentary Southfarthing ale or Sackville’s cider — allow time for a drink here before leaving, as the Inn itself is a beautifully finished set piece. Photography tip: the widest view of the Shire (looking downhill toward the lake and Mill with hobbit holes in the foreground) is best captured from the upper path early in the tour before the group spreads out.

Book at hobbitontours.com ↗ well in advance — November peak season sells out weeks ahead. The “Breakfast in the Shire” add-on (available separately, typically 9 am start) is worth considering if available when you book.

Midday

After the Hobbiton tour ends at the Green Dragon (~11:30 am), have a quick lunch before driving to rafting. Options: the Green Dragon Café serves food on-site — a pint and pie keeps it efficient. Or drive into Matamata township (~10 min) for more choice. Aim to be back on the road by 12:30 pm at the latest.

Drive south via SH29 then SH5 into Rotorua, then north to the Kaituna River put-in (~55 km from Hobbiton, ~1 hr). Kaituna Cascades and K2K Rafting both run afternoon sessions with typical starts at 1:00, 2:00, and 3:00 pm — book the 2:00 pm session when you reserve. It gives a comfortable buffer from Hobbiton and wraps by ~4:30 pm, leaving time for a shower and dinner. Arrive at the put-in 30 min early for the safety briefing. The session runs ~7 km of the Kaituna River including multiple Grade 4 rapids and the centrepiece: Tutea Falls, a 7 m vertical drop that is the highest commercially rafted waterfall in the world. Wetsuits, helmets, and paddles are provided — bring dry clothes and a bag for wet kit afterward.

Evening

Check in at the Regent of Rotorua on Hinemaru Street — a shower and change of clothes after Hobbiton and rafting are the first priorities. Aim to be seated for dinner by 7:00 pm. Atticus Finch is the right call if energy allows — it’s a 5-min walk from the hotel and the best dinner in town (see Restaurants slot). Eat Streat on Tutanekai Street is the fallback if you arrive late and just need food quickly. Pack everything tonight — bags need to be ready at the door before bed. Early start to Rotorua Airport in the morning.

Weather in November

Fiordland is one of the wettest regions on Earth — Milford Sound averages ~6–8 m of rain per year, and November sees some of the wettest conditions of the season. Expect several wet days.

Rain is not a reason to cancel Milford Sound — heavy rain dramatically amplifies the waterfalls, turning every cliff face into a cascade. Clear days are undeniably beautiful; rainy days are spectacular in a completely different way. The experience is worth it in any conditions.

Temperatures at Cabot Lodge (Manapouri): 14–19 °C (57–66 °F) during the day, cooler in the evenings. The Milford Drive runs through a steep valley — colder and mistier through the Homer Tunnel section.

Sandflies: They are genuinely relentless at Cabot Lodge, on the Milford Drive, and near any still water. Apply DEET (40%+ concentration) or permethrin-based repellent before going outdoors. This is non-negotiable — sandfly bites are immediate, intensely itchy, and persist for days.

What to pack: A proper waterproof rain jacket (essential, not optional), warm mid-layer, waterproof hiking boots or gumboots, strong insect repellent, hat and gloves for cool evenings.

Water Activities & Operators

Scuba diving:

  • Aqua-Trek Momi Bay — On-site dive centre at the Marriott. Most convenient option: direct reef access from the resort. Book through the hotel concierge. Good for certified divers wanting guided dives on the Momi Bay reef without a full-day departure commitment.
  • Subsurface Fiji ↗ — Well-regarded independent operator. Day dive trips to the Mamanuca Islands and outer reefs with smaller group sizes and experienced local guides. Book in advance for the more remote sites.
  • November conditions: Water visibility 15–25 m. Diving is best in the morning before afternoon weather develops. The Mamanuca reef system offers coral gardens, reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, and manta ray sightings on the outer reef passages.

Fishing charter:

  • Reel Fiji ↗ — Reputable sport fishing operator departing Port Denarau Marina. Full-day and half-day charters available. Target species in November: wahoo, mahi-mahi, yellowfin tuna, Spanish mackerel. Full-day (8 hrs) is worth it if fishing is a priority; half-day (4 hrs) works for a mixed-activity day.
  • Tip: Port Denarau is ~45 min from Momi Bay. Combine the fishing charter day with a marina dinner at Indigo or Rhum-Ba to make the drive count twice.

Floating bar & island day trips:

  • Cloud 9 ↗ — Fiji’s iconic floating pizza bar and infinity pool platform, anchored off Malolo Island in the Mamanucas. One of the most memorable Fiji experiences. Book in advance — it fills up. Transfers depart from Port Denarau.
  • Mamanuca day islands: South Sea Island and Beachcomber Island via South Sea Cruises (from Port Denarau). Classic Fiji beach day: snorkeling, village visit, and a Fijian kava ceremony.

Resort & Nearby Dining

At Marriott Momi Bay:

  • Talanoa — The main restaurant at the resort. Pacific Rim and international menu, open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Good quality for a resort restaurant — expect fresh fish, coconut-based dishes, and a decent wine list.
  • Goji’s Pool Bar & Grill — Relaxed poolside dining. Perfect for the beach days — grilled fish, wraps, cocktails, all while staying by the water. Ideal for Day 5 (snorkeling / beach day).

Port Denarau Marina (~45 min drive): Worth the trip on any day with a Port Denarau departure (fishing charter, day islands). Great for dinner before or after a day trip.

  • Indigo ↗ — One of Fiji’s best restaurants. Contemporary cuisine with a strong Pacific focus. Waterfront setting at the marina. Book ahead — popular with in-the-know visitors.
  • Nadina Authentic Fijian Restaurant — Excellent traditional Fijian cooking. Kokoda (Fijian ceviche), lovo-style meats, fresh tropical flavors. A standout for experiencing local cuisine properly.
  • Rhum-Ba — Lively waterfront bar at the marina. Exceptional rum cocktails, good food, and great energy for sundowners. One of the best places for a drink in the Denarau area.
  • Trader Vic’s — The original tiki bar and grill at the Sheraton, Denarau. Classic mai tais, Polynesian-style food. More of an experience than a restaurant — fun for one night, especially the cocktail menu.

Tip: Momi Bay is more secluded than Denarau, which is the point — but it means committing to a drive if you want the marina dining scene. Ideal to combine marina dinner with a day trip departure or return.

Weather in November

November marks Fiji’s transition into the wet season, but this rarely means all-day rain. The typical pattern: warm, humid mornings with excellent visibility for diving and offshore activities — then brief, heavy afternoon downpours that clear within the hour. Temperatures 27–31 °C (81–88 °F) throughout.

Ocean temperatures are warm (~27–28 °C). A 3 mm wetsuit or rashguard is sufficient for diving; most divers in November wear a shorty or thin full suit for comfort on longer sessions.

UV intensity is extreme at this latitude — apply broad-spectrum SPF 50+ sunscreen every morning and reapply after water activities. Use marine-safe (reef-safe) sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystems where you’ll be diving and snorkeling.

What to pack: High-SPF reef-safe sunscreen, rashguard, light breathable clothing, reef-safe insect repellent for evenings, sandals. A lightweight rain poncho is worth bringing for afternoon showers on Denarau days.
