Travel Logistics

Depart: Friday, November 6, 2026

Recommended flight: Air New Zealand NZ2 — JFK → AKL non-stop (~17.5 hrs). Departs JFK Terminal 1 in the evening (~10:55 pm). Crosses the International Date Line and arrives Auckland Sunday morning, Nov 8 (~6:15 am local time).

Alternative: Air New Zealand via LAX — NZ5 (JFK→LAX, ~5.5 hrs) then NZ1 (LAX→AKL, ~12.5 hrs). Adds a stopover but gives a shorter first leg and access to the Air NZ Los Angeles lounge at Tom Bradley Terminal.

Terminal: JFK Terminal 1. Arrive 2–3 hrs early. Air New Zealand has a lounge in T1. Priority Pass lounges are also accessible for Chase Sapphire Reserve and Amex Platinum cardholders.

Auckland arrival → Waiheke ferry: Auckland International Airport → Downtown Ferry Terminal (~45–60 min). Options: SkyBus express (~NZD 24 pp, drops at Britomart/Ferry Terminal), taxi (~NZD 65–80), or pre-booked private transfer. Budget 2–3 hrs from touchdown to ferry boarding at Pier 1, Quay Street.

Waiheke ferry: Fullers360 from Pier 1, Downtown Ferry Terminal → Matiatia Wharf, Waiheke Island. ~35 min crossing. Ferries run every 30–60 min. Buy tickets at fullers.co.nz ↗ or at the terminal kiosk.

Notes & Tips

Recommended routing: Air New Zealand operates a non-stop JFK → AKL service (flight NZ2, ~17.5 hrs). Departures are typically evening from JFK Terminal 1; you cross the International Date Line and arrive Auckland on Sunday morning. This is the smoothest option — no connection stress, no domestic transfer.

Alternative: Air New Zealand via LAX (NZ5 JFK→LAX, then NZ1 LAX→AKL). Adds a stopover but gives a slightly easier first leg and access to Air NZ’s LAX lounge.

Lounge: Air New Zealand has a lounge in JFK T1. Priority Pass is accepted at several JFK lounges (Chase Sapphire Reserve / Amex Platinum). Worth arriving 2–3 hrs early to decompress before a flight this long.

Long-haul tips: Set your watch to Auckland time on boarding. Prioritize sleep on the first half of the flight. Drink water consistently — not alcohol. Compression socks and a neck pillow make a real difference. Keep key items (passport, immigration card, pen) in a jacket pocket for landing.

Arrival note: You land Auckland, clear customs and biosecurity, then make your way to the Downtown Ferry Terminal for the Waiheke ferry (~35 min). Budget 2–3 hrs from touchdown to ferry boarding.

Home on Waiheke • 2 Nights

Waiheke Island, Auckland
Sun, Nov 8 – Tue, Nov 10 2 Nights

Location Notes

Waiheke is your soft landing: wine, views, low friction. No rental car needed here. Best approach is ferry + local transport.

Travel Logistics

  • Auckland Airport → Ferry Terminal: SkyBus express (~1 hr, ~NZD 24 pp) drops directly at Britomart, a short walk from Pier 1. Alternatively, taxi (~NZD 65–80, ~45 min) or pre-booked transfer. SkyBus is the most straightforward option with luggage.

  • Departure (Nov 10): Ferry from Matiatia back to downtown Auckland. Aim for a mid-morning departure (~9–10 am) to allow time to pick up the rental car and drive to Raglan without rushing.

Restaurant Guide

  • The Oyster Inn ↗ — Iconic beachside bistro at Oneroa. Known for freshly shucked oysters, ceviche, and a serious natural wine list. Book ahead — it fills up. Best for a long leisurely lunch or dinner.
  • Mudbrick Vineyard & Restaurant ↗ — Hillside vineyard restaurant with sweeping views across the Hauraki Gulf toward Auckland. Perfect for a vineyard lunch with estate wine. Romantic and unhurried. Book ahead.
  • Tantalus Estate ↗ — Award-winning winery restaurant in a striking contemporary building. Seasonal menu with matched estate wines. One of the island’s most polished dining experiences.
  • Casita Miro ↗ — Spanish-inspired tapas restaurant set among olive groves. Beautiful, relaxed setting — great for a leisurely lunch with shared plates and the house sangria.
  • Three Seven Two — Casual, friendly bistro in Oneroa village. Lower-key than the vineyard restaurants — a reliable option for a solid dinner without the need to book far in advance.

Tip: For the wine touring day, aim for 3–4 vineyard stops maximum. Tantalus and Mudbrick work well as a pairing — both have great food and don’t require driving between wineries to be a full half-day.

Weather in November

Late spring transitioning to early summer — Waiheke is one of New Zealand’s sunniest spots. Daytime temperatures 19–23 °C (66–73 °F) with typically 7–8 hours of sunshine daily. UV is high from the start of November; sunscreen every morning is essential.

Occasional afternoon showers are possible but rarely prolonged. Evenings are mild and long — daylight stretches to around 8:30–9 pm, making outdoor dining and evening vineyard visits feel relaxed rather than rushed.

What to pack: Light layers for evenings, high-SPF sunscreen and sunglasses for wine-touring days, comfortable walking shoes. A compact umbrella is handy but rarely essential.

Notes & Tips

Keep Day 1 intentionally light to absorb the travel day. Good evening to stay close to the property.

Keep the wine day curated rather than overpacked — three to four stops is the right pace.

Restaurant Guide

  • The Shack ↗ — A Raglan institution. Relaxed café serving hearty breakfasts, smoothie bowls, and good coffee throughout the day. The kind of place the whole town seems to end up at. Perfect for a morning fuel-up before surfing or exploring.
  • Raglan Wharf Kitchen & Bar ↗ — Casual waterfront dining right on Raglan’s historic harbour wharf. Fresh seafood, fish & chips, and easy-going views across the water. Great for a relaxed lunch or an easy dinner — the setting does most of the work.
  • Ulo’s Kitchen — A small, laid-back local eatery with a loyal following. Wholesome food, friendly service, and no fuss. A good option when you want something simple and low-key close to the village centre.
  • Volvo Café ↗ — A beloved Raglan breakfast and brunch spot with a slightly hippie surf-town charm. Strong coffee, great food, and the kind of mellow energy the whole town runs on. Worth a visit on Day 2 before the surf session.

Tip: Raglan is a small town — the dining scene is casual by design. Save the longer dinners for the wharf; keep breakfasts simple and local.

Weather in November

West coast exposure means Raglan runs slightly cooler and breezier than Auckland. Daytime temperatures 16–21 °C (61–70 °F), with regular offshore winds that make conditions lively for surfing.

November marks the start of reliable surf season — consistent Tasman swells arriving from the southwest. Check surf forecasts the evening before (Magic Seaweed or Surf-Forecast.com) to plan the session around tide and swell size.

What to pack: A light windproof layer for the coast. Wetsuit hire is available locally — a 3–4 mm full suit is right for November water temperatures (~17 °C). The Bridal Veil Falls trail is well shaded; a light jacket is welcome for the walk.

Notes & Tips

Good day to settle into driving on the left. Keep the evening easy.

Day 2 should feel flexible and low-pressure. Keep timing loose around surf conditions.

Regent of Rotorua • 1 Night

Rotorua, Bay of Plenty
Thu, Nov 12 – Fri, Nov 13 1 Night

Location Notes

This is a logistics-driven stop. Regent of Rotorua is a strong fit — comfortable night with better dining access and an easy airport departure the next morning. Goal: Hobbiton + rafting + early flight.

Travel Logistics

  • Raglan → Hobbiton (~1.5 hrs): Head east on SH23, north on SH1 briefly, then east on SH29 toward Matamata. Hobbiton Movie Set is ~10 min from Matamata town. Recommended operators: Kaituna Cascades and K2K Rafting both run the Kaituna River and the famous 7 m Tutea Falls drop — the highest commercially rafted waterfall in New Zealand. Sessions run ~2 hrs including safety briefing. Depart Hobbiton by 12:30 pm to reach the put-in for a 2:00–2:30 pm session.
  • Rafting → Regent of Rotorua (~20 min): The Regent is on Hinemaru Street in central Rotorua — easy to find. Expect to arrive ~5:30 pm. Gear up for a shower, dinner, and early pack for departure.
  • Departure morning (Nov 13): Rotorua Airport (ROT) is ~20 min from the hotel. Return rental car at the airport — Budget and Avis desks on-site. Allow 1.5 hrs before your flight. Air New Zealand operates ROT→AKL, connecting to ZQN. Confirm timing when booking — typically a morning departure to catch the Queenstown connection.

Restaurant Guide

  • Atticus Finch ↗ — Rotorua’s most reliable dinner option after a busy activity day. A well-run restaurant and wine bar with a quality New Zealand-focused menu — not flashy, but consistently good. Walking distance from the Regent of Rotorua. Book ahead to secure a table on a busy evening.
  • Eat Streat — Rotorua’s pedestrian dining alley lined with restaurants covering everything from pizza to Thai to burgers. Not glamorous, but extremely convenient if you’re arriving late from rafting and just want food fast. Good fallback after an exhausting activity day.
  • Regent of Rotorua Restaurant — The hotel restaurant is a solid on-property option if you’re too tired to go out. This is a one-night logistics stop — there’s no shame in a hotel dinner when you have a 9:30 am Hobbiton start behind you and an early flight ahead.

Weather in November

Inland and elevated, Rotorua runs slightly warmer and more humid than the coast. Daytime temperatures 17–22 °C (63–72 °F). November is generally pleasant with occasional afternoon thunderstorms that clear quickly.

Hobbiton sits in Waikato farmland about 1 hour from Rotorua — green and photogenic in November after the spring growth. Bring sunscreen and a hat for the outdoor tour. Rafting on the Kaituna is a full-contact water activity; weather matters less once you’re in the river — pack a warm layer for after.

What to pack: Sunscreen and hat for Hobbiton. The rafting operator provides wetsuits and helmets — bring a change of dry clothes for after the session.

Notes & Tips

One of the most time-sensitive days of the trip. Keep lunch efficient and avoid adding extra stops.

Have everything packed before bed. Hobbiton reservation at 9:30 am sets the pace for the whole day.

Queenstown

QT Queenstown • 5 Nights

Queenstown, Otago
Fri, Nov 13 – Wed, Nov 18 5 Nights

Location Notes

Queenstown is the activity hub of the trip. This is the most physically ambitious stretch. Build in weather flexibility, especially around the heli day.

Travel Logistics

  • Arrival transfer (Nov 13): Queenstown Airport (ZQN) is ~10 min from town. Uber is available in Queenstown. Taxis wait outside arrivals. Super Shuttle runs shared transfers. QT Queenstown Hotel is on Stanley Street — a short drive from the airport, walking distance to the lakefront.
  • Ben Lomond hike (Day 2 — Nov 14): Walk or drive to the Skyline Gondola base station on Brecon Street. Take the gondola up (~NZD 32) to save 400 m of elevation gain — the hike from the top to the Ben Lomond summit is ~4 hrs return, well-marked. Start by 8 am to avoid afternoon cloud and crowds. Bring layers — the summit can be windy and cold even in November.
  • Mt Cook heli hike (Day 3 — Nov 15): Recommended operators: Over The Top Helicopters (OTT) and Heliworks both run Mt Cook / Tasman Glacier heli-hike experiences from ZQN. Full-day, weather-dependent — book well ahead and ask about the rebooking policy. OTT’s “Heli Hike” program lands on the Tasman Glacier with a guide for a 2–3 hr guided walk before the return flight over the Southern Alps. One of the highlights of the South Island.
  • E-bike wine tour (Day 4 — Nov 16): Operators: Around The Basin and Queenstown Wine Trail both depart from central Queenstown and cover 4–5 Gibbston Valley wineries. Full-day tours include bike hire, a guide, and winery stops with tastings and lunch. Gibbston Valley is ~20 min east of town via SH6 — most tours handle transport for you.
  • Roy’s Peak (Day 5 — Nov 17): Drive to Wanaka (~45 min via SH6). Roy’s Peak trailhead is at Lake Wanaka Station on Mount Aspiring Road, ~7 km north of Wanaka town. 16 km return, ~5 hrs. Start early (7 am) to beat both the crowds and midday heat. The zigzag saddle viewpoint is the iconic photo spot — allow time to linger.

Restaurant Guide

  • Rātā ↗ — The standout fine dining experience in Queenstown. Chef Josh Emett’s elegant flagship in a beautifully converted colonial building on Marine Parade. Seasonal New Zealand produce, a carefully curated wine list, and service that matches. Book well ahead — this is the dinner for a special night.
  • Public Kitchen & Bar ↗ — Relaxed all-day dining at Steamer Wharf on the lakefront. Quality local produce, a broad menu that works for lunch or dinner, and excellent lake views. The right level of polish without being fussy — a reliable Queenstown staple.
  • Blue Kanu ↗ — Polynesian fusion dining with a lively, tropical atmosphere on The Mall. Bold Pacific Rim flavours, inventive cocktails, and an energetic room. Great for arrival night or the post-wine-tour evening when you want something fun rather than formal.
  • Nest — A locally loved neighbourhood restaurant with a focus on wood-fired cooking and seasonal ingredients. Intimate and unhurried — a good choice for an evening when you want something more low-key than Rātā but more considered than a pub meal.
  • Soda — A stylish cocktail bar and late-night spot in the centre of town. Less of a dinner venue and more of a wind-down destination — good cocktails and a buzzy vibe for sundowners or a nightcap after dinner elsewhere.

Tip: Book Rātā well in advance — it’s one of the most sought-after tables in the South Island. For the hike days, Public Kitchen & Bar is the easiest option: waterfront, no fuss, and works without a reservation.

Weather in November

Mountain weather is the defining feature of Queenstown’s November climate. In town: 15–20 °C (59–68 °F), often warm and clear by mid-morning. On exposed ridgelines (Ben Lomond summit, Roy’s Peak): expect temperatures 5–10 °C colder with significantly more wind.

Queenstown averages around 7–8 rain days in November — typically brief showers rather than sustained rain. Conditions on the same day can shift from overcast to brilliant sunshine within the hour.

Hike-day protocol: Check MetService’s mountain forecast each morning. The heli hike operator will advise on Go/No-Go — same-day rebooking is standard when conditions don’t cooperate. Build flexibility into Days 3 and 5 in particular.

What to pack: Warm mid-layer for the mountains (fleece or light down jacket), waterproof shell, high-SPF sunscreen for exposed ridgelines. Trekking poles are optional but helpful on Ben Lomond’s steeper sections.

Notes & Tips

Build in weather flexibility, especially around the heli day. Keep Day 3 and Day 5 adaptable in case a rebooking window opens.

Ben Lomond (Day 2) and Roy's Peak (Day 5) are the two big hike days — spread them apart for recovery.

Te Anau & Off-Lodge Dining

Cabot Lodge meals are included and excellent — the lodge team cooks dinner each evening. The options below are useful for the Milford Sound day (pack lunch or grab something in Te Anau before departing) or if you want a change of scene one evening.

  • Sandfly Café — Te Anau’s most popular breakfast and coffee stop. Busy and cheerful — a good early start before the Milford Sound run. Named after Fiordland’s infamous sandflies.
  • Miles Better Pies — Legendary local pie shop. Grab pies to take on the Milford Drive or for a picnic at the Sound. A genuine Southland institution.
  • The Fat Duck — Casual pub-style restaurant in Te Anau town centre. Reliable comfort food and a decent selection of local craft beer. Good for a no-fuss evening meal.
  • Lakeland Hotel Restaurant ↗ — Overlooks Lake Te Anau. A step up from pub dining, with a more formal menu. Handy if the lodge is a quieter night and you want a change.

Tip: Pack snacks and drinks for the Milford Sound drive — it’s ~2 hrs each way and the only food options en route are at the Homer Tunnel shelter (limited). Miles Better Pies the night before or morning of is the move.

Weather in November

Fiordland is one of the wettest regions on Earth — Milford Sound averages ~6–8 m of rain per year, and November sees some of the wettest conditions of the season. Expect several wet days.

Rain is not a reason to cancel Milford Sound — heavy rain dramatically amplifies the waterfalls, turning every cliff face into a cascade. Clear days are undeniably beautiful; rainy days are spectacular in a completely different way. The experience is worth it in any conditions.

Temperatures at Cabot Lodge (Manapouri): 14–19 °C (57–66 °F) during the day, cooler in the evenings. The Milford Drive runs through a steep valley — colder and mistier through the Homer Tunnel section.

Sandflies: They are genuinely relentless at Cabot Lodge, on the Milford Drive, and near any still water. Apply DEET (40%+ concentration) or permethrin-based repellent before going outdoors. This is non-negotiable — sandfly bites are immediate, intensely itchy, and persist for days.

What to pack: A proper waterproof rain jacket (essential, not optional), warm mid-layer, waterproof hiking boots or gumboots, strong insect repellent, hat and gloves for cool evenings.

Notes & Tips

Milford Sound cruise: Real NZ (formerly Real Journeys) and Go Orange are the main cruise operators. The 2-hour cruise covers the main fiord and viewpoints; the 3.5-hour nature cruise adds the underwater observatory. Self-driving the Milford Road is strongly recommended over a coach — the highway itself is a highlight.

Milford Drive timing: Depart Cabot Lodge by 7:30–8:00 am to arrive by ~10:00 am, targeting a mid-morning cruise when light is best and before the coach crowds arrive. Stop at Mirror Lakes and The Chasm en route. Pack lunch and snacks — the only food on the Milford Road is the Homer Tunnel shelter.

Fly fishing: Fiordland is one of the world’s premier fly fishing destinations. Cabot Lodge arranges full-day guided experiences on private backcountry rivers, with helicopter access to remote beats available. Let the lodge know your experience level so they can match the guide and location accordingly.

Departure on Nov 21: Cabot Lodge (Manapouri) is approximately 2 hours from Queenstown Airport. For an early flight to Fiji, plan to depart the lodge no later than 7:00–8:00 am.

Water Activities & Operators

Scuba diving:

  • Aqua-Trek Momi Bay — On-site dive centre at the Marriott. Most convenient option: direct reef access from the resort. Book through the hotel concierge. Good for certified divers wanting guided dives on the Momi Bay reef without a full-day departure commitment.
  • Subsurface Fiji ↗ — Well-regarded independent operator. Day dive trips to the Mamanuca Islands and outer reefs with smaller group sizes and experienced local guides. Book in advance for the more remote sites.
  • November conditions: Water visibility 15–25 m. Diving is best in the morning before afternoon weather develops. The Mamanuca reef system offers coral gardens, reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, and manta ray sightings on the outer reef passages.

Fishing charter:

  • Reel Fiji ↗ — Reputable sport fishing operator departing Port Denarau Marina. Full-day and half-day charters available. Target species in November: wahoo, mahi-mahi, yellowfin tuna, Spanish mackerel. Full-day (8 hrs) is worth it if fishing is a priority; half-day (4 hrs) works for a mixed-activity day.
  • Tip: Port Denarau is ~45 min from Momi Bay. Combine the fishing charter day with a marina dinner at Indigo or Rhum-Ba to make the drive count twice.

Floating bar & island day trips:

  • Cloud 9 ↗ — Fiji’s iconic floating pizza bar and infinity pool platform, anchored off Malolo Island in the Mamanucas. One of the most memorable Fiji experiences. Book in advance — it fills up. Transfers depart from Port Denarau.
  • Mamanuca day islands: South Sea Island and Beachcomber Island via South Sea Cruises (from Port Denarau). Classic Fiji beach day: snorkeling, village visit, and a Fijian kava ceremony.

Resort & Nearby Dining

At Marriott Momi Bay:

  • Talanoa — The main restaurant at the resort. Pacific Rim and international menu, open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Good quality for a resort restaurant — expect fresh fish, coconut-based dishes, and a decent wine list.
  • Goji’s Pool Bar & Grill — Relaxed poolside dining. Perfect for the beach days — grilled fish, wraps, cocktails, all while staying by the water. Ideal for Day 5 (snorkeling / beach day).

Port Denarau Marina (~45 min drive): Worth the trip on any day with a Port Denarau departure (fishing charter, day islands). Great for dinner before or after a day trip.

  • Indigo ↗ — One of Fiji’s best restaurants. Contemporary cuisine with a strong Pacific focus. Waterfront setting at the marina. Book ahead — popular with in-the-know visitors.
  • Nadina Authentic Fijian Restaurant — Excellent traditional Fijian cooking. Kokoda (Fijian ceviche), lovo-style meats, fresh tropical flavors. A standout for experiencing local cuisine properly.
  • Rhum-Ba — Lively waterfront bar at the marina. Exceptional rum cocktails, good food, and great energy for sundowners. One of the best places for a drink in the Denarau area.
  • Trader Vic’s — The original tiki bar and grill at the Sheraton, Denarau. Classic mai tais, Polynesian-style food. More of an experience than a restaurant — fun for one night, especially the cocktail menu.

Tip: Momi Bay is more secluded than Denarau, which is the point — but it means committing to a drive if you want the marina dining scene. Ideal to combine marina dinner with a day trip departure or return.

Weather in November

November marks Fiji’s transition into the wet season, but this rarely means all-day rain. The typical pattern: warm, humid mornings with excellent visibility for diving and offshore activities — then brief, heavy afternoon downpours that clear within the hour. Temperatures 27–31 °C (81–88 °F) throughout.

Ocean temperatures are warm (~27–28 °C). A 3 mm wetsuit or rashguard is sufficient for diving; most divers in November wear a shorty or thin full suit for comfort on longer sessions.

UV intensity is extreme at this latitude — apply broad-spectrum SPF 50+ sunscreen every morning and reapply after water activities. Use marine-safe (reef-safe) sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystems where you’ll be diving and snorkeling.

What to pack: High-SPF reef-safe sunscreen, rashguard, light breathable clothing, reef-safe insect repellent for evenings, sandals. A lightweight rain poncho is worth bringing for afternoon showers on Denarau days.

Notes & Tips

Marriott Resort Momi Bay. Momi Bay is ~45 min south of Nadi Airport — more secluded than Denarau but still a reasonable drive to Port Denarau for day trips.
