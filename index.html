Restaurants & Dining

Mudbrick Restaurant & Vineyard Fine Dining • Vineyard

Hilltop restaurant with sweeping Hauraki Gulf views. Estate-grown wines, refined NZ cuisine. The lunch tasting menu is the move here. Book ahead — this fills up.

Tantalus Estate Winery Restaurant • Modern NZ

Beautiful property — lush gardens, excellent wine, and one of the best kitchen teams on the island. Great for a long, leisurely wine-country lunch.

The Oyster Inn Coastal Bistro • Seafood

Waiheke's most celebrated restaurant. Outstanding raw bar, natural wine list, and a relaxed beachy vibe. The oysters and ceviche are non-negotiable.

Casita Miro Spanish • Vineyard Setting

Spanish-inspired tapas among the vines. Warm, convivial atmosphere — perfect for a relaxed afternoon of grazing, wine, and people-watching.

Three Seven Two Café • Casual Brunch

The local's brunch pick — unpretentious, consistently good. Ideal for a morning before hitting the vineyards.

Weather in November

New Zealand spring — expect 18–22°C, longer days, and mostly fine weather with occasional sea breezes. It can be showery; bring a light layer for the ferry. The vineyards are lush and green at this time of year.

Notes & Tips

Keep Day 1 intentionally light to absorb the travel day. Good evening to stay close to the property.

Keep the wine day curated rather than overpacked — three to four stops is the right pace. Tantalus or Mudbrick for lunch, Oyster Inn for evening drinks — that's a strong Waiheke day.

Weather in November

Waikato coast in spring — 17–21°C. Raglan is one of the most consistent surf spots in NZ year-round. November brings moderate, well-formed swells — good for all skill levels. Wind can pick up in the afternoon; mornings are typically the cleaner surf window.

Notes & Tips

Good day to settle into driving on the left. Keep the evening easy.

Day 2 should feel flexible and low-pressure. Keep timing loose around surf conditions — if the morning session runs long, let it.

Weather in November

Bay of Plenty in spring — 16–21°C. Rotorua can be misty in the morning due to geothermal activity — atmospheric but not cold. Hobbiton sits in open farmland; bring a light jacket for the early tour. Rafting is a go in almost any weather.

Notes & Tips

Restaurants & Dining

Rātā Fine Dining • Flagship NZ Cuisine

The benchmark Queenstown restaurant. Chef Martin Bosley's flagship — beautiful, ingredient-driven NZ cuisine in an elegant heritage building. Reserve well in advance. The perfect post-hike celebration dinner.

Botswana Butchery Steak & Wine • Lakefront

Spectacular setting on the Queenstown waterfront with lake and mountain views. Excellent aged beef, serious wine list. Smart-casual and one of the best dinner settings in town.

Blue Kanu Pacific Fusion • Evening Only

Polynesian-Pacific fusion with a relaxed, warm vibe. Inventive cocktails and sharing-plate-style mains. A fun departure from the typical NZ dining scene.

Public Kitchen & Bar Modern Casual • All-Day

Great for a post-activity lunch or easy dinner. Consistent, well-executed and relaxed — the workhorse restaurant for the Queenstown stretch.

Nest Intimate • Small Plates

A quieter, more intimate option — good for an evening when you want something low-key but still excellent. Strong seasonal menu.

Weather in November

Southern Alps in spring — 10–20°C with significant variation. Snow can still linger on the peaks (spectacular backdrop). Days are long and light but mountain weather is changeable — always bring a windproof layer on hikes. Ben Lomond and Roy's Peak are both fully accessible by November. Heli conditions are best in calm, clear mornings.

Notes & Tips

Build in weather flexibility, especially around the heli day. Keep Day 3 and Day 5 adaptable in case a rebooking window opens. Most Queenstown helicopter operators offer same-day rebooking if conditions are poor.

Ben Lomond (Day 2) and Roy's Peak (Day 5) are the two big hike days — spread them apart for recovery. Roy's Peak from Wānaka is a 16km return with 1,200m elevation gain — start early.

Restaurants & Dining

Primary dining is at Cabot Lodge. On any days you pass through Te Anau:

Cabot Lodge Lodge Dining • Included

Expect high-quality, intimate lodge dining in a spectacular setting. The lodge experience is a major part of the stay — dinners are unhurried and the wine selection is strong.

Redcliff Restaurant & Bar Te Anau • Best in Town

The best standalone restaurant in Te Anau — rustic stone interior, local venison and lamb, strong NZ wine selection. Worth a stop if passing through town for Milford Sound prep.

Sandfly Café Te Anau • Casual / Coffee

Great for a pre-cruise coffee and a quick breakfast. The name is a local joke — Fiordland sandflies are legendary. Pack repellent.

Miles Better Pies Te Anau • Legendary Pies

A Fiordland institution. Grab a pie for the road — genuinely one of the best things you'll eat on the trip. Venison, lamb, and cheese & bacon are the classics.

Weather in November

Fiordland is one of the wettest places on Earth — that's what makes it spectacular. In November expect 10–18°C, with a mix of clear days and heavy rainfall. The rain actually enhances Milford Sound: hundreds of temporary waterfalls cascade down the sheer cliff faces. Pack a proper rain jacket regardless of the forecast. Sandflies are active whenever there's no wind — repellent is essential.

Notes & Tips

This stretch is intentionally slower than Queenstown. Let the lodge and landscape do the work.

Fly fishing in Fiordland is genuinely world-class — remote mountain rivers, crystal-clear water, large wild brown trout. Pack light layers and waterproof boots. The guide will handle the rest.

Milford Sound road from Te Anau is ~2 hrs each way. Depart early to beat coach tour crowds. The Homer Tunnel (single-lane, give-way) is a spectacle in itself.

Activity Operators

Subsurface Fiji Scuba Diving • Port Denarau

The premier dive operator for the Denarau / Mamanuca area. PADI 5-star facility. The Coral Gardens and Gotham City dive sites off Momi Bay are outstanding — soft coral gardens in clear, warm water. Book a 2-dive day package.

Blue Water Charters Fishing Charter • Full Day

Fiji's offshore fishing is world-class — yellowfin tuna, wahoo, mahi-mahi, and billfish in season. Full-day charter departs Port Denarau. November is excellent for pelagic fishing. Book at least 2 weeks in advance.

Cloud 9 Floating Bar & Restaurant • Day Trip

Fiji's most iconic floating platform — moored off the Mamanuca Islands with a restaurant, bar, and pizza oven. Day trip from Port Denarau includes snorkeling and access to the platform. One of the most fun days possible in Fiji.

South Sea Cruises / Yasawa Flyer Island Day Trips • Snorkeling

Runs fast-ferry day trips to the Mamanuca and Yasawa islands. Beachcomber Island (45 min from Port Denarau) is the best snorkeling day trip — incredible reef, crystal water, and a beachside BBQ lunch.

Frigates Passage / Cloudbreak Surfing • Advanced Breaks

Cloudbreak (off Tavarua Island) is one of the world's most famous left-hand reef breaks. Frigates Passage is the more accessible alternative. Surf charter operators run daily transfers during swell. November can deliver excellent 3–6ft surf.

Restaurants & Dining

Marriott Resort Momi Bay — Navo Restaurant Resort Dining • Beachfront

The resort's signature restaurant — Fijian-influenced cuisine with fresh seafood. Perfect for a special evening in the Fiji stretch. The overwater setting at sunset is hard to beat.

Indigo Restaurant Port Denarau • Indian & Asian

Excellent Indian and Asian fusion in Port Denarau marina. Good break from resort dining — the butter chicken and seafood curries are outstanding.

Hemingways Brewing Company Port Denarau • Craft Beer & Casual

Great for a lunch stop or a pre-dinner drink at Port Denarau. Good burgers, local craft beers, lively marina atmosphere.

Cloud 9 Floating Platform • Pizza & Cocktails

The floating bar also serves excellent wood-fired pizza. Meals at Cloud 9 are part of the day-trip experience.

Weather in November

Fiji in late November is the edge of the dry season transitioning to the warm season — 26–30°C, mostly clear, warm water (28°C), and excellent visibility for diving and snorkeling. Occasional afternoon rain showers are brief and warm. November is one of the best months to visit: pre-peak crowds, post-southeast trade winds, and ideal ocean conditions.

Notes & Tips

Momi Bay is ~45 min south of Nadi Airport — more secluded than Denarau but still a reasonable drive to Port Denarau for activity departures and day trips.

Return routing: NAN → LAX → JFK/EWR on Nov 26. The NAN–LAX leg is approx 10 hrs; factor in the international date line — you effectively gain a day heading eastbound. Build in a buffer for the LAX connection.

Bula culture: Fijian hospitality is genuinely warm. Lean into it — this is the decompression end of the trip. No itinerary guilt here.
