Notes & Tips

Airline options: Air New Zealand (preferred — direct service JFK → LAX → AKL, Dreamliner fleet, excellent Business Premier flatbeds) or United codeshare on the same routing via Star Alliance. Air New Zealand also flies via Houston (IAH) depending on season — check availability both ways.

Typical routing: JFK → LAX (~5.5 hrs), then LAX → AKL (~12.5 hrs). Total travel ~22–24 hrs including layover. Arrive Auckland Sunday morning, losing Friday night to the date line.

Lounges: United Club at JFK (Terminal 7) pre-departure. Air New Zealand International Lounge at LAX (Tom Bradley) during layover.

Long-haul tips: Book an aisle seat for the LAX–AKL leg. Melatonin at departure helps reset. Bring a neck pillow and noise-cancelling headphones. Hydrate aggressively — pressurized cabin at altitude is dehydrating on a 12+ hr flight.

November Weather

Southern Hemisphere spring in Otago. 15–22°C on good days, dropping to 5–10°C overnight. Weather is highly changeable — clear mornings can cloud over by afternoon. The Remarkables and Ben Lomond summit may still have snow, which is spectacular from town.

Hiking: Start Ben Lomond and Roy’s Peak early (6–7 am) to summit before clouds build. Both are exposed above the treeline — bring an insulating layer even on warm days. Check MetService NZ the evening before each hike day.

Heli day: November is good for the Mt Cook glacier run. Book with a flexible rebooking policy — operators typically rebook within 24–48 hrs on weather cancellations.

Pack: Layering system essential — merino base, insulating mid-layer, waterproof shell. Hiking boots, headlamp for early starts.

Notes & Tips

Build in weather flexibility, especially around the heli day. Keep Day 3 and Day 5 adaptable in case a rebooking window opens.

Ben Lomond (Day 2) and Roy's Peak (Day 5) are the two big hike days — spread them apart for recovery.

November Weather

Fiordland is one of the wettest places on Earth. 10–18°C in November. Rainfall is frequent but fast-moving. Crucially: Milford Sound is more dramatic in rain — the waterfalls multiply. Don’t be deterred by wet weather on the cruise day.

Milford Sound road: The Homer Tunnel approach is spectacular. Allow ~2.5–3 hrs from Te Anau / Manapouri. Check NZTA road conditions before departure — the road occasionally closes in high rainfall.

Fly fishing: November is excellent for trout. Water clarity is typically good before the summer tourist influx. The Waiau River runs near Cabot Lodge.

Pack: Full waterproof layers (jacket + trousers), merino base, gumboots or waders for fishing. Insect repellent is non-negotiable — Fiordland sandflies are aggressive near water.

Notes & Tips

This stretch is intentionally slower than Queenstown. Let the lodge and landscape do the work.

Fly fishing day is one of the most unique experiences of the trip — pack accordingly and keep the evening low-key.

November Weather

Shoulder of wet season — warm, humid, with afternoon showers. 27–31°C. November brings occasional afternoon thunderstorms that clear quickly. Mornings are almost always sunny and calm — ideal for diving and fishing. The sea feels like a warm bath.

Water visibility: 20–30m on the Mamanuca reefs in November. Coral cover is strong before cyclone season proper (Jan–Mar). Excellent diving conditions.

Cyclone note: November is early in the cyclone window; risk is low but worth monitoring closer to the trip. Fiji Met Service is the reliable local source.

Pack: Light linen / resort wear throughout. Rash guard for snorkeling and diving. Reef-safe sunscreen is essential — standard sunscreen is banned at many Fiji dive sites and reef areas.

Notes & Tips

Marriott Resort Momi Bay. Momi Bay is ~45 min south of Nadi Airport — more secluded than Denarau but still a reasonable drive to Port Denarau for day trips.

Base return routing: NAN → LAX → JFK/EWR. Allow for the possibility of an overnight buffer if connection timing is messy.
